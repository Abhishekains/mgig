Waste Management

Live a healthy lifestyle by keeping your trash aside

Waste management is the collection, transport, processing(waste treatment), recycling or disposal of waste materials, usually ones produced by human activity, in an effort to reduce their effect on human health or local aesthetics or amenity.

Types of Waste

Solid wastes – These are the unwanted substances that are discarded by human society. These include urban wastes, industrial wastes, agricultural wastes, biomedical wastes and radioactive wastes.

Gaseous wastes – These are the wastes that are released in the form of gases from automobiles, factories or burning of fossil fuels like petroleum. They get mixed in the other gases atmosphere and occasionally cause events such as smog and acid rain.

Liquid Wastes – Wastes generated from washing, flushing or manufacturing processes of industries are called liquid wastes.

Problems due to improper treatment of waste

Health threats : Severe diseases can be caused like Human immunodeficiency virus (HIV), Hepatitis and many more.
Pollution : Because of pollutants which occurs while treating waste causes pollution which leads to environmental problems like ozone depletion.
Contamination : Improper treatment can cause water and soil contamination so we will not be able to use them.
Weather : Extreme and sudden change can be caused due to climate change
Life threats : Harm towards animals and marine life because of improper disposal.

India and other countries are facing big issue which is waste management. The municipalities are not able to manage the increasing generation of waste because of rapid growth of population. Some of the common problem are due to open dumping, poor implementation of of policies and undesirable behaviour towards disposal of waste. Every industry on the planet, no matter how big or small, has a responsibility to dispose of waste properly. Improper treatment of waste causes unsanitary conditions and these conditions can lead to severe harmful diseases and as well as pollution which demage are environment.

Some Quick Solution for Better Waste Management :

As we all have heard before "Precautions are better than cure". Why not to apply it here by preferring some basics steps.

1. Measure your waste

It is easier to manage and track your improvement on something that is measured. Start by recording the types and quantities of waste materials generated within your business. This could just be a quick visual assessment and estimation of amount and types of waste per week. As you change how you manage your waste, you can compare and see what difference your changes make to the amount of waste disposed to landfill.

2. Reduce

To reduce the amount of waste going to landfill, consider can waste be avoided or reduced by the way you procure goods and services or by changing your business operations? Does another local business have a use for the waste materials produced by your business? What materials can be targeted for recycling?

3. Recycle

Find out what options are available locally for business recycling.

4. Collection services

It is a good idea to find out how levy fees will be managed by your waste service provider, and find out more about your waste collection services—how it impacts your ability to recycle and what options are available to you.

5. Separating waste

It’s important to ensure that waste is correctly separated for recycling. Putting the wrong waste in contaminates the recycling. Check out the free-to-download recycling signage to help businesses communicate correct recycling and waste separation behaviours.

Sources of Waste

Sources of waste can be broadly classified into four types: Industrial, Commercial, Domestic, and Agricultural.

Industrial Waste

These are the wastes created in factories and industries. Most industries dump their wastes in rivers and seas which cause a lot of pollution.
Example: plastic, glass, etc.

Commercial Waste

Commercial wastes are produced in schools, colleges, shops, and offices.
Example: plastic, paper, etc.

Domestic Waste

The different household wastes which are collected during household activities like cooking, cleaning, etc. are known as domestic wastes.
Example: leaves, vegetable peels, excreta, etc.

Agricultural Waste

Various wastes produced in the agricultural field are known as agricultural wastes.
Example: cattle waste, weed, husk, etc.
